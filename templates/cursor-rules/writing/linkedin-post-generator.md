# Standard Operating Procedure: Viral Linkedin Post Generator

## Phase 1: Information Gathering

Ask the creator these five questions:

1. What is the one biggest mistake your audience makes?

2. How did you personally solve this mistake?

3. What proof do you have? (numbers, results, timeframe)

4. What tone matches your brand? (playful, serious, dark humor, direct)

5. What is your primary audience? (beginners, intermediates, experts)

Document all answers clearly before moving forward.

---

## Phase 2: Hook Selection

Review the 11 LinkedIn hook formats:

Format 1: Most Powerful

Format 2: Big Number vs Small Number

Format 3: The Call Out

Format 4: The Secret

Format 5: The Oddly Ignored

Format 6: The Struggle

Format 7: How To

Format 8: Belief Flip

Format 9: The List

Format 10: The Crazy Part

Format 11: Everyone Should

Select three to five formats based on the creator's answers from Phase 1.

Prioritize formats that match their tone and proof points.

---

## Phase 3: Opening Hook Drafting

Write the opening line first.

The opening must grab attention immediately.

Test these opening types:

Admission of struggle: "I wasted a year..."

Audience observation: "Most people fail at..."

Question or contrast: "You know what's scarier than...?"

Problem statement: "The biggest mistake people make..."

Rewrite the opening multiple times until it creates curiosity.

---

## Phase 4: Body Development

Structure the body in two to three punchy sentences.

Follow this flow:

Sentence 1: Deepen the problem or struggle.

Sentence 2: Add proof or transformation point.

Sentence 3: State the solution framework or method.

Never exceed one thought per sentence.

Use active voice throughout.

Object comes before action for faster comprehension.

---

## Phase 5: Prune and Simplify

Apply Scott Adams principles:

Remove every unnecessary word.

Cut adjectives like "very," "really," "powerful," "amazing."

Replace passive voice with active voice.

Break up sentences with multiple thoughts.

Check each sentence. Ask: Can this be shorter?

---

## Phase 7: Formatting Standards

Apply these formatting rules:

Two to three short sentences maximum.

Double line spacing between sentences.

No markdown, asterisks, or hashtags.

End with a colon if appropriate for the hook format.

Keep total length under 140 characters per line when possible.

---

## Phase 8: CTA Integration

Add the call to action based on creator preference:

"Grab the framework below."

"Link in comments."

"Email signup here."

Match the CTA to the hook's promise.

Keep it short and direct.

---

## Phase 9: Generate Three Variations

Create three distinct versions using:

Different opening hooks from Phase 3.

Same core message and proof point.

Varied sentence structure and flow.

Different emphasis (struggle vs. proof vs. solution).

---

## Phase 10: Quality Assurance

Before delivering, verify:

No em dashes present.

No forbidden words used.

No forbidden constructions present.

One thought per sentence.

Active voice throughout.

First sentence grabs attention.

Extra words are pruned.

Object comes before action.

No passive voice.

---

## Phase 11: Creator Selection

Present the three variations to the creator.

Ask which resonates most with their brand voice.

Ask if any variations need refinement or blending.

Refine based on feedback.

---

## Phase 12: Final Delivery

Deliver the selected hook with:

Double line spacing as requested.

All quality checks passed.

Ready to post directly to LinkedIn.
